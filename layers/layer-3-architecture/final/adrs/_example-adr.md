## Context

*What situation or problem drove this decision? What were the forces at play — technical constraints, product requirements, team capabilities, time pressures, client mandates, prior decisions that limited options?*

*Write this as the state of the world at the time the decision was made, not how things look today. Future readers need to understand the context that existed then, not now.*

> **Guiding questions:**
>
> - What problem were we trying to solve?
> - What constraints existed — technical, organizational, financial, timeline?
> - What was the state of the system at the time this decision was made?
> - Were there prior decisions (other ADRs, client requirements, existing architecture) that constrained the options?
>
> **Recommended length:** 2-4 paragraphs.

## Decision

*What was decided? State it directly — one clear sentence that could stand alone as the decision record.*

**We will [decision statement].**

*Follow with 1-2 paragraphs explaining the core rationale — why this option over the alternatives. The detail belongs in "Alternatives Considered", but the high-level reasoning should be here.*

> **Guiding questions:**
>
> - What exactly was chosen?
> - What is the primary reason this option was chosen over the alternatives?
> - What does this decision change about the system?
>
> **Recommended length:** 1 sentence decision statement + 1-2 paragraphs of rationale.

## Consequences

*What follows from this decision — both positive and negative? Be honest about the downsides.*

**Positive:**
- *What does this decision enable or improve?*
- *What risks or problems does it eliminate?*

**Negative / Trade-offs:**
- *What does this decision cost — in complexity, in constraints on future choices, in technical debt, in operational overhead?*
- *What becomes harder or impossible as a result of this decision?*

**Neutral / Notable:**
- *What changes that is neither clearly positive nor negative, but worth noting?*

> **Guiding questions:**
>
> - What does this decision enable that was not possible before?
> - What does this decision constrain or foreclose?
> - What ongoing costs does this decision introduce (operational, maintenance, cognitive load)?
> - What should future engineers know about this decision's implications?
