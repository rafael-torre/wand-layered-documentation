## Summary

K9 Wand operates with near-total public anonymity at the individual level. David DeKarske is the only named person associated with the company anywhere on the public web — appearing in a single image caption on the k9wand.com homepage with no title stated. The company website, all product pages, social profiles, app store listings, and the Google Play developer account surface only corporate entity names (WAND Holdings LLC, WAND Industries LLC, "Wand Technologies") and a single contact email ([email protected]). No team page, no LinkedIn company page, no press coverage, and no job postings exist.

Based on public records aggregators (treat as directional), David Allen DeKarske is a Michigan-born industrial product designer, born June 1, 1971, currently based in Lancaster, SC. His ~25-year career spans automotive manufacturing and product development at companies including Stant Manufacturing, RSC Bio Solutions, Matcor Metal Fabrication, and Oerlikon (Global Account Manager until ~July 2018). He co-invented automotive tow-hitch apparatus patents in ~2010–2012 with Thomas M. Williams Jr. and Gregory S. Hopper under Williams Innovations LLC (later TeleSwivel LLC) — prior work unrelated to K9 Wand. He appears to have founded WAND after leaving Oerlikon, with the k9wand.com domain registered in November 2019.

No investors, advisors, co-founders, employees, contractors, veterinary consultants, legal counsel, or development partners have been named anywhere in the public record. The company's communication style is deliberately controlled and brand-forward, consistent with a sole founder running a pre-launch operation.

## Implications

The stakeholder map for this engagement will be extremely thin from a public-record standpoint: David DeKarske is the only person to map. This has two direct implications for the engagement:

1. **Decision-making is fully centralized.** There is no evidence of any co-founder, investor, board, or advisor who shares authority. All scope, budget, design, and technical decisions flow through one person. The engagement should be structured accordingly — no assumption of parallel approval chains.

2. **Critical stakeholder gaps must be surfaced in discovery.** Several roles that typically exist in a project of this scope are invisible: a veterinary clinical advisor (essential for Vetalogix credibility and regulatory defensibility), legal counsel (IP and FDA/USDA exposure), and whoever built or is building the app and website. These are open questions for the first client session.

## Gaps and Limitations

- **Aggregator data is unverified.** Career history, contact details, and personal background come from Radaris / OfficialUSA, which scrape LinkedIn and government databases. This data is directionally useful but should not be acted on without client confirmation.
- **SC SOS filings unresolved.** Official entity details (formation date, registered agent, member list) could not be retrieved. A manual search at [businessfilings.sc.gov](https://businessfilings.sc.gov) is the only way to confirm these.
- **No internal org data.** The company's actual internal structure — contractors, advisors, collaborators — is not publicly visible. The client will need to disclose this directly.
- **LinkedIn inaccessible.** DeKarske's LinkedIn profile (if active) was not reachable via public search. A direct connection or InMail may surface additional professional context.
- **No WAND-specific patent record.** The company claims "multiple United States Patents" on k9wand.com but no pet/dog product patent under DeKarske's name was found. These may be filed under the LLC name, pending, or under a non-public assignee.
