## Summary

K9 Wand (legal entities: WAND Holdings LLC / WAND Industries LLC) is a South Carolina–based, pre-launch consumer hardware company designing premium, patent-protected dog leashes and accessories under the WAND® product line. The company positions itself as an engineering-forward challenger to a stagnant commodity market, using materials and mechanical precision more typical of aerospace or industrial design than pet products. Products are listed on their WooCommerce website with prices, but no confirmed customer reviews, retailer listings, or shipping records exist anywhere on the public web — consistent with their stated 2026 launch date.

Alongside the hardware, the company is building WANDVERSE™, a planned digital ecosystem for canine health, services, scheduling, social, and medication management. WANDVERSE has five announced pillars, one of which is Vetalogix — an AI health analytics engine described on the website as a "proprietary closed-loop Agentic RAG Engine." As of April 2026, WANDVERSE does not exist as a live product. The only digital artifact confirmed as operational is a companion Android app (WAND HALOS / com.wandapp) for the NFC-embedded HALOS collar product.

The company has a minimal public footprint: no LinkedIn company page, no Crunchbase profile, no third-party press coverage, and a nascent Instagram account with 43 followers and 3 posts. David DeKarske is the only individual named anywhere in connection with the company (credited on the homepage image), with a background in industrial design and engineering. No other team members, investors, or partners are identified publicly.

## Key Findings

### Company Identity

- **Legal names:** WAND Holdings LLC (used in manufacturing/about disclosures) and WAND Industries LLC (used in warranty/copyright pages). K9 Wand is the consumer-facing brand name.
- **Location:** South Carolina, USA. The company states "100% American. Headquartered and operated in South Carolina." No specific city is disclosed.
- **Founding date:** Not publicly stated. Earliest datable content on the site is blog posts from September 13, 2023. Copyright reads "WAND INDUSTRIES 2026."
- **Key person:** David DeKarske is the only individual name surfaced — credited on a homepage image. Background includes industrial design study at Ferris State University (1989–1993) and prior work in automotive/manufacturing. Not explicitly titled as founder or CEO on the website.
- **Tone and language:** Engineering-forward, precision-focused, premium/luxury. Uses terminology like "Anatomical Precision," "Aerospace Foundations," "Frictionless Precision," "Permanent by Design," "Post-Commodity Era." No casual or playful pet-brand register.
- **Charity arm:** K9WISHES — described as partnering with "local shelters, service dog providers, and rescue groups," operating under WAND Holdings LLC. The dedicated page (k9wand.com/k9wishes/) was returning 404 at time of research.
- **No press coverage, LinkedIn company page, or Crunchbase profile found.**

## Implications

- **Vetalogix is one pillar of WANDVERSE, which is one pillar of K9 Wand.** The Vetalogix AI symptom-checker being scoped in the technical analysis sits within a much broader planned ecosystem. Understanding the company's pre-launch status is important for framing the engagement — K9 Wand is building its core business simultaneously with its AI health product.
- **The company is pre-revenue.** No confirmed customers or transactions exist yet. This affects assumptions about existing user data, feedback loops, or operational constraints.
- **David DeKarske appears to be the sole decision-maker.** There is no named technical team, no engineering staff visible, and no external development agency credited anywhere on the site. The engagement team is likely working directly with the founder.
- **The website's existing tech stack (WordPress/WooCommerce on Bluehost) is far below the infrastructure described in the Vetalogix technical analysis (GCP, Vertex AI, BigQuery, VPC perimeters).** The AI platform being scoped would represent a major architectural leap from the company's current digital infrastructure.
