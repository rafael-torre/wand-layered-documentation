## Company Identity and History

K9 Wand is the consumer-facing brand of WAND Holdings LLC / WAND Industries LLC, a South Carolina–based company designing and manufacturing premium dog hardware under the WAND® product line. The company positions itself as an engineering challenger to a pet hardware market it describes as "stagnant — trapped in a cycle of redundant designs, basic utility, and non-specialized materials for over a century." Its central positioning statement is "WAND®: Engineering the Post-Commodity Era."

The company's founding date is not publicly disclosed. The earliest datable content on their website is from September 2023, and the copyright reads "WAND INDUSTRIES 2026." David DeKarske is the only individual identified in connection with the company — credited on the homepage and traceable through public records as having a background in industrial design, engineering, and automotive/manufacturing sectors (Ferris State University, 1989–1993). No other team members, investors, or advisors are named anywhere in public materials. As of April 2026, K9 Wand is pre-launch: products are listed on their website with prices, but no confirmed customer reviews, retailer listings, or shipping records exist anywhere on the public web. The company has no LinkedIn company page, no Crunchbase profile, and no third-party press coverage.

## Business Model

K9 Wand's current business model is direct-to-consumer (D2C) physical hardware, sold through their own WooCommerce-powered website. Their product line consists of six items: the WAND Housing (retractable leash system, $149.99), K2 (dual-dog coupler, $64.99), LUX (light module, $59.99), NEXUS (wrist attachment, $59.99), OSSO (hand attachment, $59.99), and HALOS (NFC-embedded modular collar, price not listed). There is no third-party retail channel, Amazon presence, or wholesale program found. No subscription or recurring revenue model is currently in place.

Alongside the hardware business, K9 Wand is building WANDVERSE™ — a planned digital ecosystem with five pillars: Information (GPS-indexed K9 destinations via Google Maps), ServiceSync (scheduling for grooming, daycare, and vet consultations), K9 Social Nexus (community and messaging platform), Health (Vetalogix AI engine and K9 Passport health records), and Medications (pharmaceutical price comparison). WANDVERSE is not yet live — no login portal, subscription pricing, or active platform feature exists as of April 2026. The AI health pillar, Vetalogix, is described on the company's website as a "proprietary closed-loop Agentic RAG (Retrieval Augmented Generator) Engine" — this is the feature being scoped in the current engagement.

## Customer Segments

K9 Wand's stated primary audience is modern dog owners who treat their dog as "an integral part of the family" (verbatim from About page). The company is exclusively B2C with no wholesale, veterinary, breeder, or professional sales channel identified. Product language for the K2 references "high-drive handlers" and working/sport dog use cases, suggesting a secondary appeal to serious dog sport enthusiasts. No customer testimonials, reviews, or case studies exist on any platform — consistent with the company's pre-launch status.

## Organizational Structure and Culture

Based on all available public information, K9 Wand operates as a very small organization — likely founder-led. David DeKarske is the only named individual associated with the company across the website, public records, and any searchable source. There is no published team page, no LinkedIn company page, and no named engineering, design, or operations staff. No external agency, contractor, or development partner is credited anywhere on the site.

The company's communication style is highly controlled and brand-forward — all public-facing content reflects a single, consistent voice: technical, premium, and engineering-focused. Decision-making appears centralized given the absence of any named collaborators. The culture signals a founder-inventor model: the About page references "a mechanical pencil, paper, drafting table, and passion" as the origin of the product, and the company name WAND is presented as a proprietary symbol rather than a team or brand collective. The pace of public activity is slow — 3 Instagram posts and no press coverage — consistent with a pre-launch, founder-operated company.

## Existing Systems and Technology Landscape

K9 Wand's current digital infrastructure is lightweight. The website runs on WordPress 6.9.4 with Elementor Pro as the page builder and WooCommerce 10.7.0 for e-commerce, hosted on Bluehost shared hosting behind Cloudflare for CDN and DDoS protection. Jetpack 15.7.1 is loaded as a plugin (forms, sharing, analytics). No analytics platform (Google Analytics, Facebook Pixel), payment processor scripts (Stripe, PayPal), or CRM/email marketing tool was detected in the page source.

The only confirmed mobile artifact is the WAND HALOS Android app (package ID: `com.wandapp`, developer: "Wand Technologies" on Google Play), which serves as a companion app for the HALOS NFC collar — enabling pet profile storage and passive GPS tracking via NFC scanning.

The technology stack planned for WANDVERSE (Google Maps API, an AI RAG engine, image-based health sensing, encrypted social messaging, centralized health records) represents a significant leap above the company's current infrastructure. None of these systems are live or confirmed as in active development beyond what is described on the website.
