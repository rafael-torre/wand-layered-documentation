# Business Overview

A comprehensive view of who the client is, how they operate, and what their current reality looks like. This is the first document anyone on the team should read when joining an engagement.

---

## Company Identity and History

> **Guiding questions:**
>
> - What does this company do in one sentence?
> - How long have they been around?
> - What is their size (employees, revenue, locations)?
> - How do they describe themselves — what language and tone do they use?
> - What defines their culture?
>
> **Recommended length:** 1-2 paragraphs.

---

## Business Model

> **Guiding questions:**
>
> - How does this company make money?
> - What is their primary offering — products, services, or both?
> - Who pays them?
> - What is their pricing model?
> - What does their value chain look like?
>
> **Recommended length:** 1-2 paragraphs.

---

## Customer Segments

> **Guiding questions:**
>
> - Who are their customers?
> - How does the client segment them (by industry, size, geography, behavior)?
> - Which segments are most important to their business?
> - Are there segments they are trying to grow into?
>
> **Recommended length:** 1 paragraph. Use a table if the client has clearly defined segments.

## Organizational Structure and Culture

> **Guiding questions:**
>
> - What are the key teams or departments we will interact with?
> - How are decisions typically made — centralized or distributed?
> - What is their pace of change — do they move fast or go through heavy approval cycles?
> - Are there cultural norms that affect how we work with them?
>
> **Recommended length:** 1-2 paragraphs.

---

## Existing Systems and Technology Landscape

> **Guiding questions:**
>
> - What systems does the client currently use?
> - What is their tech stack?
> - What integrations exist between systems?
> - Are there systems they are happy with, frustrated by, or actively replacing?
> - What vendor relationships are in place?
>
> **Recommended length:** 1-2 paragraphs. Use a table for a multi-system landscape.
