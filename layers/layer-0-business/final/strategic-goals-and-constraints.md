# Strategic Goals and Constraints

What the client is trying to achieve, why this engagement exists, and what limits what we can do. This document captures the forces that shape every downstream decision — from product definition to architecture to delivery.

---

## Strategic Goals

> **Guiding questions:**
>
> - What is the client trying to achieve at a business level?
> - Are these goals measurable? What does success look like to them?
> - Which goals are highest priority?
> - How do these goals connect to the engagement we are delivering?
>
> **Recommended length:** 3-5 bullet points with context, or 1-2 paragraphs.

---

## Engagement Drivers

> **Guiding questions:**
>
> - Why does this engagement exist? What triggered it?
> - What changed that made this project necessary?
> - What happens if they do nothing?
> - Is there urgency — a deadline, a competitive threat, a regulatory date?
>
> **Recommended length:** 1 paragraph.

---

## Constraints

> **Guiding questions:**
>
> - What regulations or compliance requirements apply?
> - What is the budget envelope?
> - What are the hard deadlines?
> - What organizational limitations exist (team size, skills, change capacity)?
> - What technical mandates are non-negotiable (vendors, platforms, integrations)?
>
> **Recommended length:** One row per constraint. Aim for completeness over brevity — a missing constraint discovered late is more costly than a long table.
