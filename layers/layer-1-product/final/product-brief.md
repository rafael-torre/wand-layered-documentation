# Product Brief

The alignment document for the product. This is the first thing anyone reads to understand what we're building, for whom, and why. It should be readable by any stakeholder — including non-technical clients — and concise enough to stay useful (aim for 1-3 pages).

The Product Brief does not define individual features — that happens in the [Feature Specs](features/). This document defines the product-level context that all feature specs inherit from.

---

## Problem Space

> **Guiding questions:**
>
> - What problem are we solving?
> - Who has this problem? How widespread is it?
> - What is the cost of this problem remaining unsolved — to the user, to the business?
> - Is this a new problem or an existing one that's poorly addressed?
>
> **Recommended length:** 1-2 paragraphs.

---

## Target Users

> **Guiding questions:**
>
> - Who are the primary users of this product?
> - Who are secondary users (administrators, managers, support staff)?
> - How do these users relate to the customer segments identified in Layer 0?
> - What distinguishes our users from the general population — what makes them specific?
>
> **Recommended length:** 1-2 paragraphs. Reference personas from intermediate artifacts if they exist.

---

## Product Vision

> **Guiding questions:**
>
> - What does this product do, in one paragraph?
> - What is the desired end state — what does the world look like when this product succeeds?
> - How is this different from what exists today?
>
> **Recommended length:** 1 paragraph.

---

## Scope

> **Guiding questions:**
>
> - What is in scope for this product?
> - What is explicitly out of scope — what might someone assume is included but is not?
> - What is deferred to later phases? Why?
> - Are there scope boundaries driven by constraints from Layer 0 (budget, timeline, regulatory)?
>
> **Recommended length:** 1 paragraph for what's in scope. A bulleted list for what's out of scope or deferred, with brief rationale for each.

---

## Key Assumptions

> **Guiding questions:**
>
> - What are we assuming to be true that, if wrong, would change the product direction?
> - Are there assumptions about user behavior, market conditions, technical feasibility, or business priorities?
> - Which assumptions have been validated, and which are still untested?
>
> **Recommended length:** 3-5 bullet points with enough context to understand why each assumption matters.

---

## Relationship to Business Goals

> **Guiding questions:**
>
> - How does this product connect to the strategic goals defined in Layer 0?
> - Which business objectives does it directly serve?
> - How does success for this product translate into success for the client's business?
>
> **Recommended length:** 1-2 paragraphs.
