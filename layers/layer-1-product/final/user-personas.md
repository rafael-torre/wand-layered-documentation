# User Personas

The people this product serves. Each persona represents a distinct user type with different goals, pain points, and relationships to the product. Personas are referenced throughout the product layer — feature specs name which personas they serve, user journeys are scoped to specific personas, and success metrics tie back to user outcomes.

Downstream layers also depend on personas: designers use them to guide interaction patterns (Layer 2), architects consider them for access patterns and performance targets (Layer 3).

Define one persona per distinct user type. Most products have 3-5 personas. If the list grows beyond that, some personas may be too granular — look for types that can be merged or represented as variations of a primary persona.

---

> **For each persona, copy the structure below.** Separate personas with a horizontal rule (`---`).

## [Persona Name]

### Identity

**Role / Title:** *What they do professionally, or their relationship to the product.*

**Context:** *Where are they when they use the product? What is their environment, team size, technical proficiency?*

> **Guiding questions:**
>
> - What is a typical day like for this person?
> - How tech-savvy are they?
> - What is their organizational role — decision maker, end user, administrator?

### Goals

> **Guiding questions:**
>
> - What is this person trying to accomplish with the product?
> - What does success look like for them?
> - What are their short-term goals vs. long-term goals in this domain?
>
> **Recommended length:** 3-5 bullet points.

### Pain Points

> **Guiding questions:**
>
> - What frustrates this person today?
> - What problems do they face that the product could solve?
> - What workarounds are they currently using?
> - What has failed for them in the past?
>
> **Recommended length:** 3-5 bullet points.

### Behaviors

> **Guiding questions:**
>
> - How does this person currently handle the problem space?
> - What tools do they use today?
> - How often do they encounter the problem?
> - What is their decision-making process?
>
> **Recommended length:** 1-2 paragraphs.

### Relationship to the Product

> **Guiding questions:**
>
> - How does this persona interact with the product — daily user, occasional user, administrator?
> - Which product areas or features matter most to them?
> - What would make them stop using the product?
> - How do they evaluate whether the product is working for them?
>
> **Recommended length:** 1 paragraph.
