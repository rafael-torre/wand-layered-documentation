# Success Metrics

Product-level KPIs and how we measure whether the product is succeeding. These are outcomes, not outputs — they measure the impact of the product on users and the business, not the volume of features shipped.

Per-feature success criteria belong in the individual feature spec's acceptance criteria. This document captures the product-wide measures that feature specs roll up into.

---

## Business Metrics

> **Guiding questions:**
>
> - Which metrics tie directly to the business goals defined in Layer 0?
> - What revenue, cost, or efficiency outcomes does this product target?
> - Are there contractual or engagement-level metrics the client cares about?
>
> **Recommended length:** 3-5 metrics. Fewer is better — too many metrics means none get attention.

## User Metrics

> **Guiding questions:**
>
> - How do we measure user satisfaction and experience?
> - What behavioral metrics indicate users are getting value (task completion rate, time to first value)?
> - What support or friction metrics should we track (support ticket volume, error rates)?
>
> **Recommended length:** 3-5 metrics.

## Measurement Approach

> **Guiding questions:**
>
> - How and when are metrics collected? Real-time, weekly, monthly?
> - What tools or systems are used for measurement?
> - Who reviews the metrics and at what cadence?
> - How are metric reviews connected to product decisions — what happens when a metric misses its target?
>
> **Recommended length:** 1-2 paragraphs.
