# Problem Statement

A focused framing of a specific problem the product needs to address. Used during early discovery to sharpen thinking before writing feature specs. One problem statement can lead to one or more feature specs, depending on how the solution is decomposed.

---

## Problem Description

*What is the problem? Describe it clearly and specifically. Avoid solution language — focus on the pain, not the fix.*

---

## Who Is Affected

*Which users or stakeholders experience this problem? Reference personas if they exist. How many people are affected and how often?*

---

## Current State

*How is this problem handled today? Manual workarounds, competing tools, ignored entirely? What is the cost of the current approach — in time, money, frustration, or risk?*

---

## Impact

*What happens if this problem remains unsolved? What is the business impact? What is the user impact? Is it getting worse over time?*

---

## Desired Outcome

*What does success look like? Describe the end state, not the solution. What would be true if this problem were fully addressed?*
